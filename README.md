# Wearonomics Lab

**Version 1.0 — Research Prototype**

Wearonomics is a research framework designed to validate wearable movement data and translate verified human activity into structured analytical signals.

The system explores how human-generated data can be recognised through transparent economic attribution, where validated movement produces measurable credit aligned with real-world behaviour.

---

## Research Problem

Wearable devices generate continuous behavioural data, yet:

- data is rarely validated  
- activity is often misclassified  
- individuals are not recognised for the value they generate  

Wearonomics addresses this gap through validation-first analytics and ledger-based attribution.

---

## Core Pipeline

Wearonomics operates through the following structure:

Wearable Data  
↓  
Validation Engine  
↓  
Movement Segmentation  
↓  
Anomaly Detection  
↓  
Economic Attribution  
↓  
Credit Ledger  

Only validated human movement contributes to the system.

---

## Economic Attribution

A central component of Wearonomics is the introduction of credit systems linked to verified activity.

This enables:

- recognition of human-generated data  
- behavioural incentives  
- potential applications in:
  - health systems  
  - insurance models  
  - micro-credit frameworks
